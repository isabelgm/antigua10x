Can the Antigua region lead in Agriculture?

The Antigua region is blessed with one of the most amazing agricultural conditions in the world. Recent commodity price fluctuations, increased labor costs and plagues like coffee rust (roya) have made it a challenging environment. Work with our expert panel to find the 10X opportunity in agriculture.

Luis Pedro Zelaya, Bella Vista Coffee, APCA (Antigua Growers Association). As a long time coffee grower in the Antigua region, and member of the Antigua Coffee Growers Association, Luis Pedro has a unique perspective on the future of agriculture in the region.

Julio Martinez, Pomona Capital. Julio will speak to the growing AGTECH sector and how it can positively increase the 10X potential of agriculture in the region.

Alex Kronik, Caoba Farms. Alex has been leading the way to provide organic produce in the Antigua region. He brings his insights on the importance of ensuring a local, sustainable food supply and the potential for creating a tourism destination with agriculture.

Juan Bronson, Izabal Agro Forest.

10X in Style and Textiles?

The beauty and uniqueness of Guatemala´s textiles are well known, but how we turn this artisanal quality into a 10X opportunity? This impressive group of entrepreneurs has found a way to find new markets and designs to create a 10X impact.

Francesca Kennedy, Founder Ix Style. Francesca has brought Guatemala´s typical Huarache sandals to leading retailers like GAP and J Crew. Her company not only employees several hundred artisans, but ensures clean water for the community.

Arianne Engelberg, Creative and Founder The New Denim Project. Arianne turned a crisis into an opportunity, converting a challenging time in her family's textile business into a sustainable, creative solution that is providing high quality eco-friendly denim products to partners like Whole Foods Markets.

Molly Berry, Founder Luna Zorro. Molly works directly with both indigenous Mayan cooperatives and independent artisans who weave varieties of her own original designs Luna Zorro is reaching new markets with pieces that will act as a neutral base in any home.

Anita Lara, Founder UNIK´. Anita turns Guatemalan textiles into fashion forward pieces. Learn from her experience working with artisans and taking Guatemalan fashion to the world.

To achieve a 10X impact in the social space what are the next steps. Join our panel of leaders on social entrepreneurship, metrics and reach, as they work to define the future of social impact.

Philip Wilson, CEO Ecofiltro. Philip will share his experience with Ecofiltro, a social business aimed at solving the clean water problem in Guatemala.

Rebecca Sanborn, Director, Common Hope. As the new Director of Common Hope, a not for profit organization that works with more than 7,000 children and adults on education, health and housing needs, Rebecca can speak to the challenges and opportunities of working in social impact in the region.

Rosario Uriarte, Central America and Mexico Chapter Program Coordinator (ANDE). Rosario brings her experience working with the Aspen Network of Development Entrepreneurs supporting an ecosystem of entrepreneurs focusing on development.

Sarah Sterling, Pomona Impact.Sarah will share her experience working with impact metrics in the social impact space.
